2015年5月2日，广东省人民体育场。诚十杯粤鄂慈善足球赛开赛，诚十集团与富易堂联合赞助。最动人的一幕：33个来自广州社会福利院的孩子，对阵11位退役国脚——不是比赛，是大人们对孩子最温柔的陪伴。

On May 2, 2015, the Chengshi Cup Guangdong-Hubei charity soccer match was held at Guangdong Provincial People's Stadium. Co-sponsored by Chengshi Group and FYT, organized by Guangdong Celebrity Football Club. The highlight: a heartwarming 33-vs-11 exhibition match — 33 children from Guangzhou Social Welfare Institute against 11 veteran national football players.

{{ end }}
{{ if eq .Language.Lang "zh" }}

曲楚良、胡志军、谢育新、林强、殷立华……这些曾经驰骋国家队的名字，那天只有一个身份：陪孩子踢球的人。广州电视台全程直播，所有善款用于福利院孤残儿童的教育援助。有些事，比赢球更值得全力以赴。

{{ else }}

Guangdong veterans included Qu Chuliang, Hu Zhijun (1994 season top scorer), Xie Yuxin, and Wang Huiliang. Hubei's roster featured Lin Qiang, Yin Lihua, and Chen Fangping. The match was broadcast on Guangzhou television. All proceeds benefited educational assistance for orphaned and disabled children at the welfare institute. This high-profile charity event tied the brand to sportsmanship, celebrity culture, and philanthropy.

{{ end }}

{{/* Slide 2: Rural China donations */}}
{{ if eq .Language.Lang "zh" }}

「那些被遗忘的孩子」——一份纪实报告的名字，也是出发的理由。团队穿上印着「把爱传出去」的T恤，装满一车食用油和大米，开进中国最偏远的山村。书包、足球、蜡笔、文具，每一样都亲手递到孩子手里。

不是汇一笔款就算了。是走泥巴路，进很少有外人到过的家门。面包车上的天府之花食用油和大米，沉甸甸的，是承诺的重量。孩子接过新书包时的笑，很轻，却比所有商业上的收获都值。

{{ else }}

"The Forgotten Children" — this documentary report's title was also the starting point of FYT's charitable mission. The team traveled deep into rural China, donating funds to schools, delivering cooking oil, rice, and daily necessities to families in need. Wearing charity t-shirts printed with "Spread the Love," they crossed mountains to hand school bags, footballs, crayons, and stationery directly to children.

This was not remote donation — it was hands-on action. The team walked into remote villages, along muddy paths, to homes that rarely see outside visitors. Vans loaded with Sichuan specialty cooking oil and rice were tangible expressions of the brand's social commitment. The smiles on children's faces when receiving new school bags and stationery are a return no commercial success can replace.

{{ end }}
